Fused polynomial u16 RGB gamut kernel vs matlut
Date: 2026-04-23
zenpixels-convert: commit 4171ca46 + temporary fused_poly refactor
linear-srgb: local perf/simd-tf-magetypes (has srgb_u16_to_linear_v3
             AND linear_to_srgb_u16_v3 rites)
Hardware: AMD Ryzen 9 7950X (Zen 4)

Setup
─────
Wrote convert_8px_u16_rgb_fused_poly — a single-pass SoA kernel that
uses the new linear-srgb SIMD polynomial rites for both decode and
encode, with mat3x3_x8 for the matrix step. No LUTs at all (no 256 KB
decode LUT, no 128 KB encode LUT).

Swapped FusedSrgbU16GamutRgb to call the new kernel. Tests pass
(all 242 lib + 23 integration). Benched via bench_t7_gamut.

Result
──────

  sRGB U16 fused gamut (P3→BT.709)
                    matlut (baseline)    fused_poly    delta
    256px           6.00 GiB/s           3.34 GiB/s    -44%
   4096px           5.93                 3.37          -43%
   1080p            4.89                 3.38          -31%

Fused poly is ~3.37 GiB/s flat across all working-set sizes —
compute-bound, not memory-bound.

Why
───

Per 8-pixel chunk the fused-poly kernel does:
  - 3× srgb_u16_to_linear_v3 (decode)
  - mat3x3_x8 (3 FMA triples = 9 FMAs)
  - 3× linear_to_srgb_u16_v3 (encode)

Each polynomial rite is:
  - 1× sqrt f32x8
  - 1× u16/f32 convert
  - 8× FMA on P coefficients (rational poly degree 4)
  - 8× FMA on Q coefficients
  - 1× f32 divide
  - clamp + blend

Per chunk: ~60 FMAs + 6 sqrt + 6 divide. On Zen 4 AVX2:
  - FMA f32x8: 1/cycle throughput → 60 cycles
  - sqrt f32x8: 1/6 cycles → 6 × 6 = 36 cycles
  - div f32x8: 1/7 cycles → 6 × 7 = 42 cycles
  - Independent, so roughly max(60, 36, 42) = 60 cycles/chunk

60 cyc/chunk at 4.5 GHz = 13.3 ns/chunk. 8 pixels × 12 bytes = 96 B.
96 / 13.3e-9 = 7.2 GiB/s theoretical ceiling. Observed 3.37 is about
half that — likely a combination of deinterleave/interleave overhead
(24 scalar loads + 24 scalar stores per chunk) and data-dependency
chain through the polynomial.

Matlut per 8-pixel chunk: 48 scalar LUT gathers (24 decode + 24
encode) + SIMD matrix + SIMD clamp/round. On Zen 4 with both LUTs
L2-resident, 48 gathers finish in 48/3 = 16 cycles + matrix
overhead. ~6 GiB/s observed matches.

Conclusion
──────────

For sRGB u16 RGB fused gamut on Zen 4 with L2 ≥ 384 KB, **matlut
wins**. The wisdom-#4 "51× poly over LUT" scenario was encode-step
only; once decode and encode polynomials BOTH run in the pipeline
the compute cost doubles and the LUT approach reclaims the win.

Refactor reverted. Keeping matlut as production.

Pivots worth considering (not this session)
───────────────────────────────────────────

1. Hybrid: matlut for the common sRGB path, poly for when the
   matrix+TF combination implies different primaries+TF pair (where
   writing a LUT per combo would explode cache footprint).
2. AVX-512 evaluation: 2× FMA throughput, 2× divide throughput.
   Polynomial compute cost would halve; poly might cross matlut.
   Not available on this CPU's Zen 4.
3. Use `linear_to_srgb_u16_slice_fast` (sqrt-LUT) for encode path
   specifically — keeps the 128 KB LUT (same as matlut encode) but
   SIMD-driven. Would need a rite-shaped version.
